Email Meter Selfservice redesign

2023 Product design, UX/UI

Unifying Scalability, Usability, and Visual Consistency

The Self-Service product was the foundation of Email Meter years ago. While its growth had been steady, it evolved organically without structured planning. Over time, multiple teams contributed to its development, resulting in fragmented code and a design that lacked consistency with the Enterprise product.

From a product perspective, we identified three key challenges:

  • Code optimization and cleanup to improve maintainability and scalability.

  • Redefining user flows to ensure a logical experience aligned with other products.

  • Updating the UI to reflect the new visual identity and create a frictionless transition between Self-Service and Enterprise.

Our primary goal was to enhance the user experience by making the tool more intuitive, visually appealing, and functionally robust. Additionally, research into the user journey revealed that many Self-Service Pro users requested Enterprise trials shortly after upgrading. This indicated that they were using Self-Service as a testing ground before proposing a company-wide adoption. Ensuring consistency between both products would streamline this transition.

New Components & Dashboard Restructuring

One of the most impactful changes was the dashboard reorganization. Previously, the information hierarchy was unclear, making data interpretation difficult. In the redesigned version:

  • We established a clear visual hierarchy, ensuring key metrics stand out.

  • The layout was optimized to prevent information overload and enhance navigation.

  • We introduced modular and scalable cards, making future iterations easier.

Data Prioritization & Visual Hierarchy

While some UI elements were purely aesthetic updatessuch as the top section containing filters and general statsothers were significantly reworked to improve visibility and relevance. Decisions were based on research data identifying which metrics were most critical for the average user.

  • Email Volume By Day: Transformed into a dual-graph visualization, allowing users to compare sent and received emails side by side in real-time. Previously, these metrics were displayed separately, requiring manual correlation.

  • Response Trends: Increased in size to emphasize its importance, particularly for Customer Support professionals. Highlighting this metric also served as a funnel for managers and high-potential Enterprise users.

  • Replies & Internal vs. External: Previously, four pie charts were used, which created visual clutter. The new design consolidates them into two, with color-coded segmentation (blue for internal, green for external/received emails) to improve clarity.

  • Volume Breakdown: Now unified into a single graph that clearly distinguishes data sources. A dropdown toggle allows switching between received and sent messages, reducing visual complexity.

A More Scalable UI with Tailwind

To address the lack of scalability, we adopted Tailwind UI as a foundation, allowing for improved consistency and easier maintenance. This approach enabled us to:

  • Implement reusable components, inspired by the Enterprise Portal.

  • Enhance visual coherence across the platform.

  • Introduce improved interactive elements such as animated buttons and refined dropdowns.

Expanded Graphs & Enhanced Interactivity

The previous version had significant shortcomings in data visualization. Graphs lacked hierarchy, and accompanying text did not always clarify data relationships. To resolve this:

  • Graph sizes were increased to emphasize key metrics.

  • Color-coded indicators were added to simplify interpretation.

  • Interactive elements now provide additional details on hover/click, reducing ambiguity.

Refining the Details: Tooltips & Microinteractions

One major pain point was the overuse of popups as tooltips. In the redesigned version:

  • Tooltip styles were standardized, ensuring a consistent and polished look.

  • Their behavior was refined to be less intrusive while remaining informative.

  • Microinteractions were optimized to create a smoother user experience.

With these changes, we also addressed previous UI issues, such as the tooltip behavior that unnecessarily dimmed the entire dashboardan overkill effect that completely nullified the subtlety of a tooltip.

Beyond the Dashboard: Onboarding & Settings Optimization

User onboarding is a crucial phase in product adoption. The redesign introduced:

  • A simplified, visually engaging onboarding flow.

  • Interactive guides to assist first-time users.

  • Improved navigation to reduce friction during initial setup.

Additionally, menus such as User Settings and Benchmark Settings were restructured for clarity. This allowed us to:

  • Simplify navigation, making complex configurations more intuitive.

  • Introduce new sections for better workspace, plan, and billing management.

  • Add in-line tips linking to the Help Center for users needing deeper explanations.

(Annotated images showcasing settings improvements)

Conclusion: A More Scalable and Cohesive Product

The Self-Service redesign transformed the platform into a more cohesive, accessible, and scalable product. With cleaner code, a structured UI system based on Tailwind, and improved user flows, we created a more intuitive experience tailored to diverse user profiles.

The dashboard restructuring and data prioritization improved usability and information clarity. The addition of interactive elements and refined tooltips reduced friction, making navigation more seamless.

Beyond usability improvements, this redesign laid the foundation for future scalability. The alignment with Enterprise UI components ensures easier cross-product transitions and minimizes future design overhead.

Ultimately, the Self-Service product is now a more powerful and adaptable tool, strategically positioned to support both individual users and growing businesses.
